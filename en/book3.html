“Find something you would be willing to die for — and when you find it, live for it.”

The Book of Five Rings is a medieval treatise on the way of the sword (kendō), written by the legendary Japanese samurai and swordsman Miyamoto Musashi (1584–1645). The book is still studied today by masters of kendō as well as by Japanese managers, who consider it a practical manual of strategy in business and in life. The edition is supplemented with Dokkōdō, accompanied by commentary from the Zen monk Sandōkai Sen.
